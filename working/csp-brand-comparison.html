Tradeoffs at a glance

Option A — the problem

Title competes with nav links for horizontal space. At 1280px and below something has to give — truncation, wrapping, or dropped CTAs. The oversized nav is sticky on every page, not just the homepage.

Option B — clean but cold

The nav is the leanest, but without any hero the homepage feels like it jumps straight to utility. Fine for returning power users; less welcoming for a first visit or a student who's never used the library before.

Option C — the best of both

Title stays in the nav at a readable 1rem (not oversized, not invisible). The hero delivers the welcome moment, the search, and at-a-glance status — all homepage-only content that scrolls away. Nav stays compact once you're past the fold.

The hero does real work

That right-side stats column isn't decorative — hours, chat availability, and tutoring status are the three most common reasons students visit the homepage. Surfacing them in the hero means fewer clicks to answer "is the library open right now?"

Subsite consistency

Subsites use the same compact nav as Option C (just swap the title for the crumb + subsite name). The welcome hero is homepage-only. The system stays coherent across all three tiers.

LibGuides implementation

The welcome hero goes in the homepage guide group's Custom Header only. The nav is system-wide. Hours and chat status can be live-fetched from LibCal and LibChat APIs to keep the stats current without manual updates.
